What's HDU?

High-Density Urethane (HDU) is a strong, naturally waterproof material engineered specifically to make signs. It is a preferred material for sign shops not only for its durability, but because it is easier than wood to cut, carve, and route, which reduces the overall cost. All of the sign companies we spoke with during the process recommended this material. HDU in signage is incredibly common. Many subdivisions and businesses have HDU signage, and you may not even realize it. Some examples include:

  • Incline Village (Foristell)
  • Good News Brewing (St. Charles)
  • 100 Miranda Court (Wentzville)

As HDU is waterproof, it is unaffected by the elements and will not rot, decompose, splinter, crack, or peel with time. It will also not swell or shrink with the weather like wood does. It can even be sandblasted to replicate wood grain, brick, or other special effects. Creative Graphic Solutions stated the vast majority of subdivisions and businesses are choosing HDU due to durability and cost. The company has replaced and upgraded a number of subdivision's signage and the original wood signs have been replaced with the HDU material (Incline Village listed above is one example). They also noted they have yet to have to do replacement or repair on signs that have been installed using this material.

Once the sign is routed, it will be painted and UV treated to resist fading. HDU signage is expected to look like new for, at minimum, 10 years. Anything left out in the elements will start to show effects from the sun over time (there is no way to 100% prevent this), and the paint can be refreshed as time goes on. A paint refresh will be the general maintenance item as the sign material itself is impervious to the elements and will not deteriorate. Most look at doing maintenance on their signage at the 20-ish+ year mark after it has started to really fade.

Swing by any of the examples listed above if you'd like to check out an HDU sign in the wild.
